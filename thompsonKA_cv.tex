% \noindent I am an evolutionary biologist whose research centres around the evolutionary ecology of hybridization. I use a combination of theory, data synthesis, and experiments to work toward identifying general features about how selection acts on hybrids in relevant ecological contexts. My empirical research uses a variety of systems, from vascular plants to fish. The overarching goal of my research is to clarify the importance of ecology-based selection (i.e., ‘extrinsic’) selection against hybrids for speciation and identify the mechanisms through which it acts.
